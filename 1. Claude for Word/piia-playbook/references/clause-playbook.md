# Clause-by-Clause Playbook

> NOT LEGAL ADVICE — informational/educational; for attorney review (see SKILL.md
> disclaimer).

Standard positions for a **new-employee** PIIA.

The playbook is in two parts:

- **Core** — the provisions that belong in every form. Always include these.
- **Add-ons** — provisions added based on the company's circumstances. Each has a
  **trigger** (the employee's work state, a multi-state footprint, or AI exposure)
  and is **mandatory or elective** when that trigger is present.

Model text is in `model-language.md`; AI terms are in `ai-provisions.md`; state
requirements and Exhibit B contents are in `state-statutes.md`. Exhibit convention:
**Exhibit A = Prior Inventions**, **Exhibit B = Inventors' Rights Statutes**.

---

# PART 1 — CORE (always include)

## 1. Parties & structure
"Company" extends to the corporate group — affiliates, subsidiaries, successors,
and assigns. Define it once and use it throughout.

## 2. Effective at hire; condition of employment
Effective on the first day of employment and supported by the offer of employment.
New-hire framing only — no "continued employment" / mid-employment consideration.

## 3. Nondisclosure / protection obligation
No use or disclosure except within the scope of employment or as authorized;
reasonable precautions and pre-publication approval; survives termination.

## 4. DTSA / 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b) immunity notice
Include verbatim; omitting it forfeits exemplary damages and attorneys' fees. Model
text in `model-language.md` §6.

## 5. Confidential Information — definition
Broad, illustrative definition covering business, technical, financial, customer,
personnel, and third-party-confidential information, and — where the AI module
applies — AI-related Confidential Information (the Company's foundation-model-provider
and AI-vendor relationships, prompts and system instructions, benchmarks and internal
evaluations, and confidential information received from AI vendors; see
`ai-provisions.md`); state that it remains Company property. Standard
provable exclusions (public domain, already known, third-party, independently
developed) are an acceptable add; do not put a fixed time limit on true trade
secrets.

## 6. Permitted disclosures / protected-activity carve-out
Nothing in the Agreement limits the employee's right to contact or file with a
government agency (SEC, EEOC, NLRB, OSHA, DOL), exercise NLRA § 7 rights including
discussing wages, report conduct reasonably believed unlawful (including
harassment or discrimination), or communicate with their attorney. Pair with the
Confidential Information definition (#5) so "Confidential Information" doesn't reach
lawful wage discussion. Model text in `model-language.md` §7.

## 7. No improper use of prior-employer / third-party information
Employee won't use or bring any third party's confidential information and hasn't
retained former-employer materials.

## 8. Inventions — defined terms
Define "Invention," "Company Invention," "Intellectual Property," and "IP Rights"
broadly (idea through code to know-how, patentable or not). Where the AI module
applies, "Invention" also reaches AI-Assisted Work Product, prompts and prompt
libraries, and agent configurations and system prompts (see `ai-provisions.md`).

## 9. Prior Inventions carve-out & disclosure → Exhibit A; license-back
Employee lists prior/background inventions on **Exhibit A** (with a "none"
checkbox); those are excluded from assignment, and the Company gets a non-exclusive,
royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, sublicensable license to any that
are incorporated into a Company product. **Always attach Exhibit A** — omitting it
is a common, avoidable defect.

## 10. Present assignment of Company Inventions
Use "I hereby assign and agree to assign" — a present assignment ("agree to assign"
alone can leave a title gap, the *Stanford v. Roche* problem). Reaches AI-assisted
inventions, AI-Assisted Work Product, and AI Output (see `ai-provisions.md`) and is always subject to the
state carve-out in Exhibit B (Add-on A).

## 11. Moral-rights waiver
Waiver/consent on moral rights "to the extent permitted by law."

## 12. Duty to disclose inventions
Prompt disclosure so the Company can assess ownership, including items the employee
believes are non-assignable. Keep any post-termination tail to disclosure only,
short, and tied to Company work.

## 13. Works made for hire
Acknowledge works are "works made for hire" under 17 U.S.C. § 101, with the present
assignment as a fallback.

## 14. Further assurances / assistance + power of attorney
Employee assists with filings during and after employment, and grants an
irrevocable power of attorney coupled with an interest if they won't sign.
Reasonable compensation for post-termination assistance is a customary add.

## 15. Facilitation — execute oaths / documents
Employee will execute documents, oaths, and declarations needed to perfect the
Company's rights.

## 16. Quitclaim / waiver of infringement claims
Employee waives and quitclaims any infringement claims against the Company in
Assigned Inventions.

## 17. No copyleft / open-source code without approval
Employee will not incorporate into any Invention, Company software, or other
deliverable — or otherwise deliver to the Company — any code under the GNU GPL,
LGPL, Affero GPL, or any other "copyleft" or reciprocal license that would require
or condition the use or distribution of such code on the disclosure, licensing, or
distribution of source code owned or licensed by the Company, except in strict
compliance with the Company's open-source / software-use policy or as directed by
the Company.

## 18. Maintenance of invention records
Employee keeps adequate invention records; records are Company property.

## 19. No-conflicting-agreements representation
Employee represents no other agreement or obligation conflicts with this Agreement
or the employment.

## 20. Return of Company property at termination
Return or destroy all materials at termination or on request, with a narrow
personal-records exception (own compensation records, shareholder materials, a copy
of the agreement).

## 21. Company property; no expectation of privacy / system monitoring
No expectation of privacy on Company systems; the Company may monitor and inspect.
Keep monitoring tied to Company-owned/managed systems.

## 22. Notice of obligations to future employers
Employee will provide a copy (or the Company may notify a future employer) so they
are aware of the employee's continuing confidentiality and IP obligations. A
notice / enforcement-support provision, not a restrictive covenant.

## 23. At-will employment
Not an employment contract for any term; employment is at-will. (Montana differs
after the probationary period — use a Montana-specific insert for MT employees.)

## 24. General / boilerplate
Include: entire agreement / merger; successors & assigns; notices; severability +
judicial reformation; equitable remedies / injunctive relief; advice of counsel;
voluntary-execution acknowledgment; counterparts / electronic signature; survival.

# PART 2 — ADD-ONS (include based on company circumstances)

Not on every form. Each is governed by a **trigger** and is **mandatory or
elective** once that trigger is present.

## A. State invention-assignment carve-out → Exhibit B
**Mandatory when triggered. Trigger: the employee's work state.** For an employee
in a carve-out state, exclude inventions developed entirely on own time, without
Company resources, and unrelated to Company work — and place the statute text in
Exhibit B. Use the express, state-cited carve-out (a generic savings clause may not
satisfy a statutory **notice** requirement); California also requires the §2870
notice in the body, and New York (Lab. Law § 203-f) is treated as a carve-out
state. See `state-statutes.md`.

## B. Flexible multi-state governing law
**Structural choice when triggered. Trigger: a multi-state footprint.** Float
governing law and forum to the employee's **primary work state**, which applies the
right state's invention-assignment rule and satisfies **Cal. Lab. Code § 925** and
**Washington** (RCW 49.62) automatically. Alternative: a fixed home-state default
with explicit CA/WA carve-outs. Model text in `model-language.md` §Governing law.

## C. AI module
**Elective. Trigger: the company uses AI tools or has AI-exposed roles.**
Approved-tools-only use, tied to the Company's Artificial Intelligence Use Policy
(the "AI Policy"); no Confidential Information into unapproved AI Tools; assignment of
AI-assisted inventions, AI-Assisted Work Product, prompts and prompt libraries, and
agent configurations and system prompts; disclosure of material AI use; and AI-related
Confidential Information (AI-vendor relationships, prompts and system instructions,
benchmarks and internal evaluations, and vendor-supplied confidential information)
protected as Confidential Information. Assumes the company builds on top of foundation
models rather than training its own. See `references/ai-provisions.md`.

## Out of scope (flag for removal if seen)

- **Restrictive covenants** — non-compete; non-solicit of employees or customers;
  duty of loyalty / non-competition during employment beyond the no-conflicts rep;
  tolling-of-restrictions; reasonableness/"earn a living" acknowledgments tied to
  covenants.
- **Consultant / advisor / contractor** terms or a "Service Relationship" framing.
- **Mid-employment / continued-employment consideration** language.
