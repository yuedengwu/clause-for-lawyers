## 1. Recital / consideration (new hire)

> "In consideration of, and as a condition of, my employment with [Company], a
> Delaware corporation (together with its affiliates, subsidiaries, successors and
> assigns, the 'Company'), and effective as of the first day of my employment, I
> agree to this Employee Confidential Information and Inventions Assignment Agreement (this
> 'Agreement')."

Do not add "or continued employment" / mid-employment consideration language —
out of scope.

---

## 2. Confidential Information — definition

> "I agree that all information, in whatever form, that is disclosed to me or to
> which I have access during my employment, and that the Company treats as
> confidential or that derives value from not being generally known, constitutes
> 'Confidential Information.' Confidential Information includes, without limitation:
> (a) trade secrets, inventions, ideas, processes, formulas, source and object
> code, data, programs, algorithms, models, and other works of authorship;
> (b) information regarding research, development, products, services, marketing,
> business and financial plans, budgets, pricing, costs, and unpublished financial
> statements; (c) information regarding customers, suppliers, investors, and
> employees, including lists and the terms of any agreements with them; and
> (d) any information that the Company has received from a third party subject to
> an obligation of confidentiality. Confidential Information does not include
> information that I can establish (i) was publicly known through no fault of mine,
> (ii) was rightfully in my possession before the Company disclosed it to me, or
> (iii) becomes rightfully available to me from a third party without restriction.
> I will hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence, will not use it
> except for the benefit of the Company in the course of my employment, and will
> not disclose it to anyone outside the Company without authorization, both during
> and after my employment."

Pair with the protected-activity carve-out (§7) and the DTSA notice (§6) so the
definition does not encompass lawful wage discussion or protected disclosures. Do not
put a fixed time limit on true trade secrets. Where the AI module applies, add
AI-related Confidential Information per `ai-provisions.md`.

## 3. Inventions / Company Inventions — definitions

> "'Inventions' means all inventions, discoveries, developments, concepts,
> improvements, designs, works of authorship, software, mask works, trade secrets,
> and know-how, whether or not patentable or registrable under patent, copyright,
> or similar laws. 'Company Inventions' means all Inventions that I solely or
> jointly conceive, develop, reduce to practice, or create during the period of my
> employment that (a) relate to the Company's business or its actual or
> demonstrably anticipated research or development, (b) result from work performed
> by me for the Company, or (c) are developed using the Company's equipment,
> supplies, facilities, or Confidential Information."

Keep the definition broad (patentable or not). The (a)/(b)/(c) scope of "Company
Inventions" deliberately mirrors the boundary of the state carve-out (§5 / Add-on
A), so own-time/own-resource/unrelated inventions fall outside it. Where the AI
module applies, extend "Invention" to AI-Assisted Work Product, prompts, and agent
configurations per `ai-provisions.md`.

## 4. Assignment of Company Inventions (present assignment + quitclaim)

> "I hereby irrevocably assign to the Company, and agree to assign, all of my
> right, title, and interest worldwide in and to any and all Company Inventions and
> all Intellectual Property Rights in them. This present assignment includes
> Company Inventions conceived or developed during my employment. I will promptly
> disclose all Inventions to the Company so it can determine whether they are
> Company Inventions. To the extent I retain any rights in Company Inventions, I
> waive and quitclaim to the Company any claims of any kind I may have against the
> Company arising from their use. This assignment is subject to, and limited by,
> the state invention-assignment carve-out in [Section __]."

Present assignment ("hereby irrevocably assign, and agree to assign" — avoids the
*Stanford v. Roche* title gap). Relies on "Company Inventions" as defined in §3, so
the (a)/(b)/(c) scope is not restated here. Folds in prompt disclosure and the
quitclaim of infringement claims. Renumber "[Section __]" to the state carve-out in
your form (it is §5 / Add-on A here).

## 5. State invention-assignment carve-out → Exhibit B

**Express carve-out (preferred; include when Company has employees in states with Inventors' Rights Statutes) — references Exhibit B:**

> "The assignment in this Section does not apply to, and 'Company Inventions' does
> not include, any Invention that I developed entirely on my own time without using
> the Company's equipment, supplies, facilities, or Confidential Information, and
> that (a) does not relate at the time of conception or reduction to practice to
> the Company's business or actual or demonstrably anticipated research or
> development, and (b) does not result from any work performed by me for the
> Company, in each case to the extent required by the Inventors' Rights Statutes
> applicable to my state of employment, as set out in **Exhibit
> B**."

**General savings clause (only for states with no specific statute):**

> "Notwithstanding anything in this Agreement to the contrary, this Agreement does not require
> assignment of any invention that is excluded from assignment under applicable law."

## 6. DTSA immunity notice (required — every PIIA)

> "Pursuant to 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b): (1) An individual shall not be held criminally
> or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure
> of a trade secret that (A) is made (i) in confidence to a federal, state, or
> local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, and
> (ii) solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation
> of law; or (B) is made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or
> other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal; and (2) an individual who
> files a lawsuit for retaliation by an employer for reporting a suspected
> violation of law may disclose the trade secret to the individual's attorney and
> use it in the court proceeding, if the individual (A) files any document
> containing the trade secret under seal and (B) does not disclose it except
> pursuant to court order."

## 7. Protected-activity / whistleblower / pay-transparency carve-out

> "Nothing in this Agreement: (i) limits my rights under Section 7 of the National
> Labor Relations Act, including to discuss wages, hours, or terms and conditions
> of employment; (ii) prohibits me from filing a charge or complaint with,
> communicating with, or participating in any investigation or proceeding of any
> federal, state, or local government agency (including the SEC, EEOC, NLRB, OSHA,
> DOJ, or DOL), or from receiving a whistleblower award, in each case without
> notice to or approval from the Company; (iii) prevents me from discussing or
> disclosing conduct I reasonably believe to be unlawful, including harassment or
> discrimination; or (iv) prevents me from communicating with my attorney. In
> making any such disclosure, I will take reasonable precautions to avoid
> unnecessary disclosure of Confidential Information."

## 8. Prior inventions (Exhibit A)

> "I will not incorporate any prior or background invention listed in **Exhibit A**
> (or any technology I own or have an interest in) into any Company product,
> process, or development without the Company's prior written consent. If I do, I
> hereby grant the Company a non-exclusive, royalty-free, fully paid-up, perpetual,
> irrevocable, worldwide license (with the right to sublicense through multiple
> tiers) to make, have made, use, import, reproduce, modify, distribute, and
> otherwise exploit such technology as part of the Company's products and services,
> but not to market it separately from the Company's products or services."

## 9. Governing law

**For multi-state forms:**

> "The validity, interpretation, construction and performance of this Agreement, and all acts and
> transactions pursuant hereto and the rights and obligations of the parties hereto shall be
> governed, construed and interpreted in accordance with the laws of the state in which I am
> employed by the Company, or, if I am a remote employee, the State in which I primarily perform
> services for the Company, without giving effect to any principles of conflict of laws that would
> lead to the application of the laws of another jurisdiction"

**For single-state forms:**

> "This Agreement will be governed by and construed according to the laws of the State of
> [State Name]. I hereby expressly consent to the personal jurisdiction and venue of the state and
> federal courts located in [City, State] for any lawsuit filed there against me by
> Company arising from or related to this Agreement."

## 10. Common boilerplate

Include, in substance:

> **Entire agreement.** "This Agreement is the entire agreement between me and the
> Company regarding its subject matter and supersedes prior agreements on that
> subject."
> **Successors & assigns.** "This Agreement binds my heirs and representatives and
> benefits the Company's successors and assigns; the Company may assign it."
> **Survival.** "My obligations survive termination of my employment for any
> reason."
> **Severability / reformation.** "If any provision is unenforceable, it will be
> reformed to the minimum extent necessary and the rest remains in effect."
> **Equitable relief.** "Breach may cause irreparable harm for which damages are
> inadequate; the Company is entitled to injunctive relief without bond, in
> addition to other remedies."
> **Advice of counsel.** "I have had the opportunity to consult counsel of my
> choosing before signing."
> **Voluntary execution.** "I have read and understand this Agreement and sign it
> voluntarily."
> **Counterparts / e-signature.** "This Agreement may be signed in counterparts
> and by electronic signature."
> **Notices.** "Notices will be in writing to the addresses on the signature page
> (or as updated)."
