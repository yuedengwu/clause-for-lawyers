# Prompt 1

Create an HTML file: a daily briefing on the latest developments in US privacy law.
Audience: an attorney looking to keep up with the latest developments.
Research first: search for significant US privacy law developments from the past month. Focus on new state laws, regulator enforcement and settlements, state AI legislation, federal activity. "Significant" means a notable change in the law or a strong signal about enforcement, not simply what is most discussed.
If a bucket has no real development this period, say so rather than padding it.
After research, create the brief as an HTML file in the format below.

 1. Header: "Daily Brief" and today's date.

 2. "At a glance" box with three sections:
- What's new: the single biggest development from the past week.
- Ongoing: a topic that has dominated the discussion for longer than a week.
- On the horizon: a coming change (e.g., effective date, a pending bill likely to pass, federal preemption).

 3. Three sections, in this order:
- State laws, including privacy and AI laws
- State enforcement actions & settlements
- Federal
Within section 1, tag each item as [Privacy] and/or [AI] so the reader sees which is which.

 Each section contains up to 3 articles. Fewer than three is fine if there’s nothing relevant; do not pad a section to reach three.

 Each item shows:
- title
- publisher
- a working link to the source article
- if the item involves a specific statute, government guidance, or bill, include . Omit this entirely when there isn't one; do not write "N/A".
- a one-line description of what it covers and why it's worth reading.

 Flag the most important items across the brief as a must-read with a star. Include 1-3 must-reads per edition.

# Prompt 2

Can you make the following changes please to the brief?
For every article listed, at the end of the article description, can you add a toggle?
When the toggle expands, I'd like to see one to three sentences about what's changed about the law and if there are any new compliance requirements for companies.
For the length, let's keep the entire brief to a five-minute read. Any descriptions, aside from the one we just talked about in the toggle that expands, should be only one to two sentences.

# Prompt 3

Please make these changes:
Compliance calendar: tracker for upcoming compliance deadlines of new regulations
Create a table with columns: date, jurisdiction, who is impacted (e.g. companies with gross annual revenue over $25M), link to statute and government guidance, 1-2 sentences on new requirements that require company action, with link to one articles that discusses this from a reputable source (e.g. alert from leading law firm).
Create a drop down menu so that users can filter the table by jurisdiction. Users should be able to check and uncheck the relevant jurisdictions, and see only the rows for the states that are selected.
Include any deadlines within 90 days of the date of the brief
For each article, remove the words “What’s Changed & Compliance Impact.” Make the toggle 30% bigger and place it at the end of the article description, inline.
Style: professional, clean, uncluttered, like a well-designed print newsletter.
Typography: serif font for the title and section headings (e.g. Georgia or a similar classic serif); sans-serif for body text, subtitles, and labels (e.g. Helvetica, Arial, or system-ui). This contrast between serif headings and sans-serif body is the core of the look.
Color: black text on a white background. No accent colors. Use weight, size, and spacing for hierarchy instead of color.
Restrained throughout: at most two or three font sizes, one or two font weights. The polish comes from spacing and typographic contrast, not decoration.
